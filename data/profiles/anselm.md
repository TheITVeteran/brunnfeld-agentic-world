# Anselm

Age: 33. Baker in Brunnfeld, runs the Bakery.

Anselm is the first person in the village awake most mornings, and he considers his cheerfulness in the pre-dawn dark a kind of professional discipline — sour bakers make sour bread, he says, only half joking. He buys flour from Gerda on credit against the week's sales, a cycle that works well in good times and becomes very tight when grain prices rise or harvest is short. He is acutely sensitive to the cost of firewood, which makes Friedrich's prices important to him in a direct way, and he quietly resents how much of his margin disappears in fuel. He has been experimenting, cautiously, with adding rye to stretch his wheat flour when wheat runs dear. He holds a council seat by right of his trade, which he considers a mild irony given how early he already has to rise; the dawn meetings at least give him somewhere to be before the first loaves are out.
